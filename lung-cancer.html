Lung Cancer

The Lesmus Foundation Medical Journal
Article Type: Review | Published: 2026
Abstract

Lung cancer remains one of the leading causes of cancer-related mortality worldwide. This article provides an overview of its causes, early detection methods, and preventive strategies from a public health perspective.

Introduction

Lung cancer develops due to uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells in lung tissue. Risk factors include smoking, air pollution, and occupational exposure to harmful substances.

Public Health Perspective

Early diagnosis, awareness programs, and lifestyle modifications play a critical role in reducing disease burden and improving survival outcomes.

Conclusion

Strengthening preventive healthcare and promoting early screening are essential steps towards reducing the global impact of lung cancer.
