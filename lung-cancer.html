Lung Cancer

The Lesmus Foundation Medical Journal
Review Article · Published 2026
Abstract Lung cancer is one of the most prevalent malignancies worldwide. This review outlines risk factors, early detection, and public health approaches aimed at reducing disease burden.

Introduction

Lung cancer arises from uncontrolled proliferation of abnormal cells in pulmonary tissue. Tobacco exposure, environmental pollutants, and genetic susceptibility are major contributors.

Public Health Importance

Preventive strategies such as smoking cessation, early screening, and awareness programs play a crucial role in improving outcomes.

Conclusion

Strengthening public health policies and promoting early diagnosis are essential for reducing lung cancer mortality.
