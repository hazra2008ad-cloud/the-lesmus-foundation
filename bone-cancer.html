Bone Cancer (Bone Sarcoma)

Medical & Educational Article • The Lesmus Foundation

Abstract

Primary bone cancer is a rare malignancy originating from bone tissue. This article provides a comprehensive overview of bone cancer, including its types, symptoms, diagnosis, staging, treatment, and prognosis, with emphasis on both primary and secondary bone cancers.

Overview

Primary bone cancer is a rare cancer that starts in the bones. It is also called bone sarcoma. There are more than 200 bones in the human body and together they form the skeleton.

If the cancer spreads into the bones from another part of the body, it is called secondary bone cancer.

What is Primary Bone Cancer?

Primary bone cancer starts in the cells of the bones. This is different from secondary or metastatic bone cancer, which spreads to the bones from elsewhere in the body.

The Bones

The Skeleton

The bones support the body, protect organs, and act as levers for muscles to allow movement. Long bones support the body, the rib cage protects the lungs and heart, and the skull protects the brain.

The Bone Cells

Bone is a framework of connective tissue hardened by minerals like calcium. There are three main bone cells:

  • Osteoblasts – form new bone
  • Osteoclasts – break down excess bone
  • Osteocytes – maintain bone structure

Bone marrow inside bones produces blood cells from stem cells.

Types of Primary Bone Cancer

  • Osteosarcoma – commonly around the knee
  • Chondrosarcoma – cartilage-forming cancer
  • Ewing Sarcoma – common in children and adolescents
  • Spindle cell sarcoma
  • Chordoma

Bone Cancer Symptoms

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent bone pain, often worse at night
  • Swelling or lump
  • Difficulty moving or limping

Less Common Symptoms

  • Pathological fractures
  • Fatigue
  • Fever
  • Unexplained weight loss

Staging and Grading

Staging describes cancer size and spread, while grading describes how abnormal the cells look. The TNM system is most commonly used.

  • Low grade: slow growing
  • High grade: aggressive, higher spread risk

Treatment

  • Surgery
  • Chemotherapy
  • Radiotherapy
  • Targeted therapy

Secondary Bone Cancer

Secondary bone cancer occurs when cancer spreads from another organ to the bones. It is also called metastasis.

Outlook and Prognosis

The outlook depends on cancer type, stage, spread, and response to treatment. Early diagnosis improves survival.
